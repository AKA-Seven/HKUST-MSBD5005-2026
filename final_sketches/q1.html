How to read the figures

Each view uses the same underlying trade graph: nodes are companies, edges are shipments with a calendar month on the horizontal axis.

  • Heatmap: rows are high-activity companies; cell color encodes how many edges they participate in that month.
  • Ridge: stacks smoothed monthly totals so you can compare who is persistently loud vs. briefly loud.
  • Bubble: x-axis is overall activity, y-axis is distinct partners; size reinforces volume.
  • Chord: arc segments are companies; ribbons are directed trade between the selected top actors for the current month.

Short windows of very high activity compared to typical months.

Active months recur at fairly regular spacing.

short_term

Most observed activity is confined to a narrow span of months.

general

Does not meet the above rules; residual catch-all category.
